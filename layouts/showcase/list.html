See It Work

PAX aren't just metadata files. When independently created PAX share constructs, Praxis automatically discovers cross-domain pathways, identifies gaps in the evidence, and synthesizes findings across research traditions that never talk to each other.

Cross-Domain Pathway Discovery

Two PAX created independently — happiness-economics and social-determinants-of-health — both define constructs like social_support and life_expectancy. Praxis discovers 5 causal pathways between them automatically.

praxis_pathway(from="social_support", to="life_expectancy")
5 paths found
Social Support → Household Income → Life Expectancy
Confidence: moderate · Crosses: Subjective Wellbeing → Social Determinants of Health · 2 hops, 4 evidence sources
Social Support → Educational Attainment → Household Income → Life Expectancy
3 hops via education and income · Both PAX contributed independently
Social Support → Life Expectancy
Direct link · Holt-Lunstad et al. (2015): social isolation increases mortality risk by 26%

Neither PAX "knew" about the other. The pathway emerged because both mapped their findings to the same construct vocabulary.

Gap Analysis: Where the Evidence Is Missing

How It Works

1.

Agent Incorporates Papers

An AI agent reads a paper, extracts constructs (theoretical concepts), findings (empirical results with directions and effect sizes), and sources. Everything is mapped to a shared construct vocabulary.

2.

PAX Packages Knowledge

Constructs, findings, engines, and playbooks are bundled into a PAX — a portable, composable unit. The PAX carries the intelligence, not the data. Data comes via acquisition protocols.

3.

Knowledge Compounds

When PAX share constructs, the knowledge graph grows. Cross-domain pathways emerge. Gaps become visible. Every new PAX makes every existing PAX more valuable.
