From civil war onset to coup forecasting: state capacity as the common thread

Three pax cover the same underlying phenomenon from different angles: Fearon & Laitin's civil war onset model focuses on structural opportunity, the coup-prediction pax maps disposition and coordination among military elites, and the state-fragility pax tracks institutional erosion over time. Each uses institutional_capacity and related measures of state power.

Aligning findings across these pax lets an agent answer questions no single pax was designed for: which fragility indicators precede both coup attempts and civil war onset — and by how many years? That lead-time signal matters for early warning systems.

// story.03

Tax structure, redistribution, and market inequality: the fiscal feedback loop

The taxation-fiscal-policy pax and the income-inequality-gini pax were authored independently, but they share the same underlying questions about how fiscal structure shapes distributional outcomes. The taxation pax covers incidence and compliance; the inequality pax covers concentration and mobility. Together they let an agent trace the full circuit: tax design → effective incidence → post-transfer Gini → intergenerational mobility.

The equity-trading-markets pax adds a third lens: how inequality-driven savings gluts and risk appetite shift asset pricing. An agent spanning all three can quantify how a 1-point Gini increase transmits to equity volatility — a question that requires evidence from all three fields simultaneously.
